Rivers

The scientific definition of rivers is that they are bodies of water that move from areas of higher elevation, such as mountains, to places of lower elevation, like oceans. They are an integral part of the water cycle, the journey of water across the Earth's surface.>

Trees

A tree is a tall plant that can live a very long time. It has a single stem or trunk and branches that support leaves. Beneath the ground, a tree has a root system that acts as an anchor and stores the water and nutrients the plant needs to grow.>

Flowers

Wildflowers are plants that grow naturally in their environment without human intervention. They are a diverse group of flowering plants found in various habitats, including meadows, forests, and wetlands. These plants play a crucial role in supporting wildlife, including pollinators, and can contribute to the overall health of ecosystems. >
